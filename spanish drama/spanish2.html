As the sun dipped below the horizon, casting hues of pink and orange across the sky, Ernesto found himself standing on the golden sands of the beach, the roar of the waves crashing against the shore drowning out the tumultuous thoughts swirling in his mind. Beside him, Esmeralda watched the ocean with a contemplative expression, her long dark hair billowing in the gentle breeze. "I've always found solace by the sea," she remarked, her voice soft and distant. "There's something about the endless expanse of water that puts everything into perspective." Ernesto nodded in agreement, his gaze fixed on the horizon as he struggled to push aside the gnawing doubts that threatened to consume him. "It's easy to forget your troubles when you're surrounded by such beauty," he said, his voice tinged with melancholy. Esmeralda turned towards him, her green eyes filled with concern. "Is everything alright, Ernesto?" she asked, reaching out to touch his arm. Ernesto forced a smile, though it felt hollow and insincere. "I'm fine, Esmeralda," he replied, his voice betraying his inner turmoil. "Just... lost in thought, I suppose." Before Esmeralda could respond, the sound of approaching footsteps drew their attention, and they turned to see Lorenco striding towards them, his presence commanding attention even amidst the natural beauty of the beach. "Well, well, well," Lorenco said, a mischievous grin playing at the corners of his lips. "What do we have here? A romantic rendezvous by the sea?" Ernesto's jaw clenched at the sight of Lorenco, his jealousy flaring anew at the easy charm and confidence that seemed to emanate from every pore of his being. "We were just enjoying the sunset," he replied tersely, his voice edged with irritation. Lorenco chuckled, unfazed by Ernesto's hostility as he sauntered closer, his gaze fixed on Esmeralda. "Ah, yes. The sunset," he said, his voice low and seductive. "A perfect backdrop for a lovers' tryst, wouldn't you agree, Esmeralda?" Esmeralda shifted uncomfortably under Lorenco's intense gaze, a flush rising to her cheeks as she struggled to find the right words. "I-I suppose so," she stammered, her voice barely above a whisper. Sensing her discomfort, Ernesto stepped forward, placing himself between Esmeralda and Lorenco, a protective instinct surging through him. "I think that's enough, Lorenco," he said, his voice cold and clipped. "Esmeralda and I were having a private moment." Lorenco raised an eyebrow in amusement, a smirk playing at the corners of his lips. "Forgive me, Ernesto," he said, his tone mocking. "I didn't realize I was intruding on your private moment." The tension between the three of them hung heavy in the air, thick and suffocating as the crashing waves pounded against the shore. Esmeralda glanced between Ernesto and Lorenco, her heart torn between the two men who vied for her affection. "I think we should go," she said softly, her voice barely audible over the roar of the waves. Ernesto nodded in agreement, his jaw clenched with frustration as he turned to leave, Lorenco following close behind.
